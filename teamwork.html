Research #1 · 2023
Research #1

"Negative Emotions Among Youth in Ea H'Leo"

Team Member Analyst

Province-level research project investigating the emotional wellbeing of young people in Ea H'Leo district. My responsibilities included survey administration, data analysis support, and report writing.

Self-reflect: Learning to speak up

My strength was listening. I enjoyed understanding different perspectives, observing patterns in the data, and helping the team make sense of information. As an Analyst, I naturally focused on details before forming conclusions.

My weakness was hesitation. I often waited until I was completely certain before sharing an idea. Some of my observations could have helped the team earlier. I learned that collaboration is not only about listening carefully, but also about sharing insights when they can create value.

This realization motivated me to take on a leadership role in my next research project.

Research #2 · Project Leader
Research #2

"AI-Based Detection of Negative Emotions for High School Students"

Project Leader Harmonizer

A research project applying AI-based emotion recognition to support high school students in Ea H'Leo. I led the research design, coordinated team activities, monitored progress, and presented to the academic board.

Self-reflect: Balancing empathy & responsibility
Moment 1: Carrying the team's workload

As deadlines approached, I took over teammates' work to keep things moving. The project advanced, but I reduced others' chances to learn. I had confused support with responsibility.

Moment 2: Facing questions I couldn't answer

The academic board asked about model performance under real-world lighting. I hadn't explored its limitations deeply enough. Good decisions require evidence, not assumptions.

Real delegation is
Assign → Trust → Support Not: Assign → Worry → Take Over
Research #3 · Team Leader
Research #3

"Robot Maze Runner"

Team Leader Analyst Harmonizer

A robotics project designing and programming an autonomous robot capable of navigating a maze efficiently. I coordinated the team, contributed to strategy, tested navigation algorithms, and supported system integration.

Self-reflect: Leading through listening

Instead of solving every problem myself, I spent more time listening to teammates' ideas before deciding. Team discussions often revealed solutions I wouldn't have found alone.

As an Analyst, data became the basis for decisions rather than intuition alone. As a Harmonizer, I ensured every member felt heard, turning disagreements about design choices into collaborative outcomes.

The biggest lesson: Leadership is not about having the most answers. It is about creating an environment where the best ideas can emerge, be evaluated logically, and be turned into action by the entire team.

Forming Stage

At the beginning, our team members were not very familiar with one another. We introduced ourselves, shared our strengths, and discussed our goals for the project. Although everyone had different skills, we realized that our abilities complemented each other well.

Storming Stage

As we started working together, disagreements arose regarding task allocation and technical solutions. To overcome these conflicts, we held discussions where everyone could share their ideas, and important decisions were made collectively.

Norming Stage

The team gradually developed a clear workflow. We agreed to hold weekly meetings, communicate regularly, respect deadlines, and support one another when challenges occurred. This improved both teamwork and efficiency.

Performing Stage

With stronger collaboration and better coordination, the team completed the project successfully. The robot performed reliably and achieved excellent results during the final evaluation.

Adjourning Stage

After finishing the project, we reflected on our experience, discussed lessons learned, and recognized each member's contributions. The project helped us improve both our technical and teamwork skills.

Research #4 · Project Leader
Research #4

"Physical Report 1: Newton's Cradle"

Project Leader

A physics investigation exploring the principles of momentum conservation and energy transfer through the Newton's Cradle model. I led the project planning, coordinated experimental procedures, supervised data collection, and guided the team in analyzing results and preparing the final report.

Self-reflect: When leadership became analytical

Compared with my previous projects, I approached this one differently. Instead of focusing primarily on task completion, I focused on understanding the reasoning behind every result.

As project leader, I encouraged team members to share observations before drawing conclusions. Some results initially seemed inconsistent with our expectations, but through discussion and repeated testing, we discovered that small measurement errors and experimental limitations influenced the outcome.

This experience strengthened my Rational decision-making style. Rather than relying on assumptions, I learned to examine evidence, question unexpected results, and adjust our explanations accordingly.

At the same time, my role as a Harmonizer remained important. Team members often had different interpretations of the data, and I found that active listening helped us evaluate ideas objectively without turning disagreements into conflicts.

One lesson stood out
Good leadership is not about proving that you are right.
It is about creating a process where the team can discover what is true together.

Through this project, I became more confident in combining analytical thinking with collaborative teamwork, allowing both the data and the people behind it to contribute to a stronger final outcome.
